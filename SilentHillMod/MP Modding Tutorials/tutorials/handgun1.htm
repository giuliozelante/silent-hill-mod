Modelling a Handgun in 3D Studio Max 5

In this tutorial, I'll be guiding you through the process of creating a simple weapon in 3D Studio Max 5. It's not going to be beautiful, and I don't really have too much time at my mates place to fiddle with Max5... but it will give you an insight into the workings of basic modelling and texturing... You should NOTE: this tutorial will ONLY cover BASIC modelling and TEXTURING. Bones and animation for weapons will be a whole new tutorial, likewise there will be a separate tutorial for implementing your weapons...

Before I begin, I'd like to share a couple of tips with you and basically... these are common sense game modding tips, any designer or 3D artist will know this stuff already but let me just remind you...

  • Keep your poly-count to an absolute minimum, but don't be tight with detail (800-1000)
  • Most details on your model can be textured instead of modelled, i.e. ridges and bumps
  • Save your work progress continuously under sequential filenames "gun1", "gun2", "gun3"
  • Don't leave texturing to the last minute, texture half way through so you can see how it looks
  • With circular shapes, stick with octagons (8 sides) or LESS, use shading/smoothing to help!
  • Don't forget to triangulate your model to help the geometry export

Naturally, for this tutorial you WILL need a copy of 3D Studio Max version 5 with the plug-ins from the Remedy Max Payne 2 Developer pack. I also really hope you have some knowledge of 3D Studio Max. As usual I have been patronising to the very, very, very last letter *cough*, so hopefully near complete n00bs to 3DsMax5 will know what I'm ranting on about...

For this tutorial, I am not providing a HOIME pack since I know my weapon will get ripped off and used in some mod no matter how good or bad it looks. Instead I am providing the base image I'm using to create my weapon from... which you should also use to follow this tutorial...

I will also warn you that this is a LONG ASS tutorial, if you don't have the patience or time to commit to it, then I suggest leaving it for the time being and getting a "3DsMax5 for dummies" book.

Intro. Ok, first things first is to grab that image I linked above, or an image of the weapon you'd like to model. I recommend a left facing image of the weapon you want laying flat. This gives you the starting point you need to begin modelling your weapon. Ok, so open up 3D Studio Max 5, and then select the VIEW menu at the top of the screen, find and select "Viewport background". You should have a dialog like this come up...

1. This setup is simple, and it will basically let you place an image as the viewport background (makes things easy to work with). So, CLICK the "Files" button (1), and then select the image file you want to use for your weapon. Make sure "Match Bitmap" (2) is selected, this will ensure that the image is never stretched or contorted. Select "Display Background" (3), and Lock Zoom/Pan (4). Ensure that "Active Only" (5) is selected, otherwise all the other viewports get the background too. Also you want to work from the "Top" viewport, not the front or left or right etc... so make sure it's selected (6). Finally, once you are done, press "Ok" (7). You're screen should look like this...

2. Now, let's start with modelling the weapon. I chose the Glock 18c for this tutorial since it's such a blocky weapon, doesn't require too much modelling detail, and it's a commonly used weapon throughout the US armed forces, military etc... so it's easily recognised in a game. Before you begin, what I recommend doing is maximizing the top viewport, the simplest way to do this is to select/click inside the TOP viewport (it will have a yellow border), then CLICK the "Min/Max" toggle button in the bottom right hand corner of the screen...

3. Ok, done that? NOW we do some modelling, cuz' I lied in the step above... I guess I'm just "jumping the gun" (har har har). Ok, so for real now... select from the creation panel "shapes". If you are a complete n00b with 3DsMax, then the creation panel is the complex looking panel on the top right hand side of the screen, just below the toolbar. You want to CLICK on the FIRST tab (which has an mouse pointer icon)... then CLICK the button with 3 shapes on it (next to the button with the sphere icon). Now you are read to create some SPLINES; so select LINE, now you can move to the next step... If you are still puzzled by what the freakin shix0r I've just done, look at the image below... Start from the first red circle and work your way down...

4. Now this is the fun part (but mommy? isn't it ALL fun? - haha, no. Wait till you get to editing the text files ). Ok, so you may have already guessed, but we're going to draw an outline of the gun. NOT the top part, just the base. Basically we are excluding the barrel, the breach, the finger/trigger guard, and the trigger. Have a look at the image below to see what I mean... so basically, you are outlining the part in green, the rest will be done separately later on - the reason for this is that 1. the breach part will need to move back and forth when shooting, 2. the finger guard is thinner than the base of the gun so it would look clunky and odd if we modelled it now. 3. The trigger again is smaller than the base. And 4. The muzzle or barrel will be revealed when the breach moves back.

To outline your weapon, basically CLICK in the TOP viewport at a starting point say the bottom end of the handle, then click all the way around. Now the trick here is to NOT click at every curve, kind of try to produce the shape of the gun, but in the least amount of clicks. The reason here is poly reduction, if I went around the gun in detail I could so easily end up with a shape that had 200+ sides, and when we extrude the shape and triangulate it, we'd have 700+ faces - and that results in ONE laggy mufo-ass gun (of course this is going to be Baaaaaad). A basic tip is to remember is; that a curve or corner can be produced with 3 clicks, not 10.

When you have gone completely around the gun, CLICK back onto your starting point and you will be asked if you'd like to "close the spline" - select yes. When you are done, you'll have an outline which looks something like this...

5. The next step here is to outline the other parts, again I stress that it is important to be detailed, but not so detailed that every curve, and corner is modelled freakin' super accurately. Start with the breach, then do the finger guard. LEAVE the trigger, and the barrel/muzzle alone.

When outlining the finger guard, do it so you leave some over-hang at either end... like this...

Now, as mentioned above, just outline the breach and then we'll move on to the next step. When you're done, you should have something resembling this...

6. From the picture above you can see already that it's looking like a gun, but it's just a pile of shitty lines until we extrude and begin tweaking it. For some of you who are really really reaaaaallly new to this shit, it will take a few attempts to get it the way you want it. It's easy to get frustrated, but if you place a point wrong or whatever you can always edit each point you placed it to make it better. Or err... start again... *DAMN YOU!!! ~ shakes fist ~*

6.1 For the benefit of those who messed up a bit during drawing the gun outline, this is how we can correct the points you placed. Before you can edit the points, you just finish up the outline and make sure you CLOSE the spline. Now select/click the outline you buggered up, and select the modifiers tab...

6.2 Now the next thing you need to do is select the "Vertex" item from the list. This means you can select points individually and move them around...

6.3 To move these "vertex" points you need to click the "MOVE" button from the toolbar, it looks like this...

6.4 Now select the vertecies you want move (they are marked by little + (crosses). One you have clicked and selected one, you are free to move it anywhere you like. Below you can see I have neatened up my trigger/finger guard.

7. Rock on Elvis (uh huhuh)... let's extrude this and make it come to life... but first SAVE! Instead of ordinary save, use "Save As" and rename the file "glock2" or whatever you have named it, basically now you will be working from "glock2" automatically, and glock1 is safe and sound.

To extrude; SELECT the main gun base outline and go to the modifiers panel (explained in step 6.1), and from the modifiers list select "extrude", you will get some new options, under "amount" enter "40.0" it's a starting size, and we can always scale it up or down if we need to later on. Do the same for all of the other shapes... except for the trigger guard, you should enter an extrude amount of "20.0" (half). Your weapon should be looking like this...

8. Ok, so looking more like a gun I think you'd agree? The next thing to do is hide the parts we aren't working on, because things will get a little complex from here onwards (yeah right, like things aren't complex now *doh!*). Select the "breach" part (indicated purple in my picture), then CLICK the "display" tab, scroll down to "HIDE" category and then CLICK "hide selected"... have a look at the picture below...

To make it more clear, here's that panel enlarged...

9. Ok, now here's a tiny... little... complex thing, go back to the Modifiers tab (come on you should know what this is by now... it's the second icon at the top of the picture above). Then,RIGHT CLICK on the Extrude modifier you added earlier, you now have a popup menu... from the menu CLICK "Collapse to", you will get a "F*CK YOU$£!£%! message box", it's ok just CLICK YES to get rid of it, the warning has no relevance here. Basically what we have done is make the mesh will absorb the modifier of "extrude" so we can mess around with it without effecting the extrude depth. REMEMBER HOW TO DO, WHAT, ERR... YOU JUST DID!

10. Ok if you thought "complex" was; 3 bisexual teens in a weird love triangle thing... then this is going to blow your mind... *cough*. Right-o, now that you've converted the base of your gun to an "editable mesh", from the same panel (modifiers), you will need to look at the list box which has the top item as "editable mesh"...SELECT "POLYGON". This will allow you to select individual faces for editing. So select all the forward facing faces/polys on the handle of the gun... (do them one at a time) like in my picture... if you find you can't select more than one poly, hold CTRL and click the next one...

11. The next step here is to do something called "slicing" this is where we literally slice the selected faces in half... (and has nothing to do with cakes or pizzas)... but why? Well it's to add some detail to our gun handle... it will help with giving that smooth and realistic look. So, from the actions panel select SLICE PLANE... look at the screenshot above, the button is on the right hand side panel midway down. Now upon clicking that you will have a yellow square/border type thing, this shows what is being cut. For the moment it's fine where it is, so just CLICK the SLICE button... you should see a change almost immediately...

Turn OFF the slice plane by clicking the "SLICE PLANE" button again, now from the modifier stack (where you selected "Polygon" from)... find and SELECT "vertex". Now the red faces should disappear and you should have some pretty looking red and blue dots (awww ain't they cute). Select the MOVE button and move these dots a tiny bit towards you... this can be more easily accomplished by placing the MOVE cursor over the RED arrow (x axis), then click and drag. But don't drag it too far out, just a little... And now your gun looks like this...

If you like you can repeat the process for the back end, but first save as "glock4" and work from that.

12. Now, there's just a few more things we need to do to tidy this up before we can work on the breach, trigger, and trigger guard. Basically we need to think about when a gun is fired the breach moves back and reveals the barrel and also the ejection hole for the bullet. So because our breach will be moving back we need to think about these factors.

Ok, so first we'll work on the barrel part, this will be attached to the main base section it will save on detail and keep things manageable. Move the camera around to look at the top of your gun where the breach would be. Now, we need to create another slice plane, but before that, select "polygon" from the modifiers stack (like you did earlier), now select the top face.Now click SLICE PLANE, and then CLICK the ROTATE button (which is next to the move button on the toolbar)... Ok, stay with me now... this is easier than you think... Ok so look along the bottom of 3DsMax screen there are 3 input boxes X, Y, and Z. In the Y box TYPE "90" this will rotate the slice plane so that it looks like it could chop the front end of the gun off... now CLICK the MOVE button and bring the slice plane along a tiny bit towards the front end of the gun... then click SLICE. I really freakin' hope your gun model looks like this so far... otherwise the lord knows I will get a billion e-mails about it *heheh*

13. Alright schveeet, looking good and no, it's not too hard from here. I'm just smacking myself because it's so difficult to explain the inner workings of 3DsMax, to people who have probably never used it before now. I'm always wondering if I need to go into more detail or whether the people who already know 3DsMax will be shouting at me for patronising them

Aside from my ramble select the bit on the end you just sliced (again using POLYGON mode from the modifiers stack). Then turn the SLICE panel OFF, and now TURN it back ON AGAIN (this resets the slice plane). Now in the those boxes I mentioned earlier (X,Y,Z) in the Z box type "16" and press SLICE, type "-32" thenpress SLICE again, and now you have something that looks like this...

14. Ok now scroll up from "Slice Panel" and there should be a button that says "extrude". It's handy at this point to make sure you have the all 4 views open so you can see how far to extrude (just click the Min/Max button)... Now this is important to note, the 2; UP and DOWN arrows next to the extrude button will create a new block/layer every time you press them, we only need to make 2 blocks/layer extensions so if you freak up, click UNDO.

Right let's do this... CLICK and HOLD on the UP button and drag UP, WATCH the model in the TOP window and stop half way to the top of the breach (on the background image)... then let go, and repeat the process once more, only this time stopping JUST underneath the top of the breach... so it looks like this...

15. Ok at this point You should SAVE. At this point you may need to weld some vertices, but again for this tutorial it's too complex... I don't want to go into it... also partly because I'm already on step 15 and I haven't even got to the breach or texturing yet! Welding is something you need a good logical mind for, and a bit of experience, it's something that you will need a separate mini tutorial for... and, this is a MAX PAYNE 2 modding site, not teach 3D Studio Max site

Anyway, from here, SELECT "edge" from the modifier stack, now select the 2 shorter edges at the top of the extruded stack... then click the SCALE button (next to rotate button on the main toolbar), now RIGHT click this BUTTON and a small dialog pops up. You have 4 boxes, 3 on one side and 1 alone on the other side ("world scale")... in the single box, TYPE "70%", this will bring the top edges together and make the top of the stack sloping, but symmetrically... like this...
