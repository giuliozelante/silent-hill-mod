By now, you're probably really sick and tired of making in-door levels. If that's the case, let's step outside for some fresh air. Outdoor scenes are so damn easy to do you'll be done and dusted in no-time! First thing you'll need to do is get yourself a skybox texture. If you don't have one of those, then you can use this one (download it here). Secondly, there is no HOIME map for this one since it's so easy. I have however included all of the skymaps available in Max Payne just below WITH the reference names you should use in the levels.txt file.

1. Alright let's move on, open up MaxED2 (as if that wasn't obvious enough). Create a new box mesh about 8x8 (meters) using F2 (creation mode). With polygon mode (F4), and CULLED turned ON... raise the ceiling to about 5 or 6 meters - this will form your skybox room. Now basically let me just explain, for this tutorial I'm showing you how to get the skybox working, not create an elaborate mountain rage with red crested golden eagles flying around ok? So in short what you'll be doing is creating 2 rooms, one which is normal, and the other which is a "skybox".

2. The next thing you should do is create a secondary room, which will be "normal", use creation mode to draw out a 2x4x3 (WxLxH) room (as in my screenshot). Union the two rooms together (F5 for object mode, click one room, press U and then click the other room), then separate the two rooms with an exit.

3. Ok, now this is where you'll need your skybox texture. Firstly if you hadn't guessed already you'll need to add the skybox category to your texture panel. To do this, right click the texture panel and select "add category". From the dialog box that pops up, select "skybox", actually if you press "S" (for skybox), it will be selected automatically. Press OK or Enter on your keyboard and the category is added.

4. Alright, now dudes (and dudettes)... do I really need to explain importing textures? Fuck it, I'll do it anyway... ok now you have you skybox category added, you'll automatically be "inside" that category. So right click the texture panel again, and scroll down to "insert bitmaps", from there find your skybox texture and select/open it. You'll then have something that looks like...

5. Can ya' guess what we're gonna do now? Rock on! We're going to apply the skybox texture to the larger room. Now, a handy thing to note at this time is that a whole room does NOT have to be covered in the skybox texture... for now we'll just do the whole room... so, press F6 (texture mode), and click on the larger room, then press "T" (which textures the whole mesh automatically). One thing that might happen to you, is that the mesh disappears. Alas, the texture is not sucky, and you didn't do anything wrong. Simply press "7" on your number pad and it is displayed. Why does this happen? Well the skybox texture is considered a "no draw material" and is hidden by MaxED2. Remember, there are a whole range of visual filters that control what can be seen and what cannot. Press F1 to bring up this list... handy eh?

6. What's left to do?!?? Nothing really, add a jumppoint to the smaller room facing the skybox, save your map, export it, and play it. Really that was it! Yup, as simple as wiping your butt after taking a dump. One thing you should know, is that; skybox textures do NOT need to be lit (GIS'ed).

Try this one out for skylights... windows can be achieved in the same way but with larger skyboxes so you can add buildings and details that are "in the distance"
