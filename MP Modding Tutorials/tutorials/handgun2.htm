16. Ohkee Dohkie... picking up from the last page... believe me we have a shit load more to go through, but hopefully after you have made this one weapon you will have the knowledge you need to experiment and make more weapons... I dread to think of how many "Look!!1!!11 My New Glock Weapon" mods there will be floating around after this tutorial... OW MY HEAD!!! STOP THE VOICES!!!

Yo! so... the next step here is to bring the length of the extruded part back so that it will cover the breach hole (when we make one). So bring the camera around to look at the back end of the part you just made, you have the view shown in the screener. Select "vertex" from the modifier stack and select all of the vertices (dots) on my screener...

17. Now DRAG these vertices back and watch the whole extruded part move with it. Uhm, just a reminder to use the MOVE tool along the X-Axis (red arrow). Use the TOP view to move this section back... I'd say to just over half of the length of the gun - or if you like; where the trigger guard meets the handle. It should now look like this...

18. So, hey? Remember earlier when we hid some parts of the gun? Well it's time to unhide them! First though you need to save-as "Glock 5". Go to the "display panel" where you hid those items, and CLICK "Unhide all"... (now if only I could do that to find my keys...). Ok so you have all your parts back, now you are reminded of just how shitty the model looks, but that will change over the course of this tutorial... I hope... *gulp*. Ok, so... you should now SELECT the trigger guard object, and in the "absolute positioning boxes" (those 3 input boxes I have mentioned a few times... X, Y, Z...) - in the Z box TYPE  "10.0" The trigger guard should be half-way up and in the middle of the handle. Like this...

19. What we're about to do is UNION or if you like FUSE the trigger guard to the handle but first... we need to make the backend of the trigger guard bigger so it transitions nicely into the handle. So turn WIREFRAME mode on in your 3D view and rotate the camera to look at the backend of the trigger guard so it looks like the screener.

Before you can proceed, you might remember from the first part that we needed to make the BASE object editable? Well we need to do the same for the trigger guard object too! So from the modifiers stack/list, RIGHT CLICK on the "extrude" item/modifier (as before) and from the menu that pops up, select "collapse to"... now select the back end of the trigger guard using POLYGON selection mode from the modifiers stack... (Look at my screener it's highlighted yellow). Select the SCALE tool and along the BLUE arrow (Z axis) drag upwards so it's slopping but still remains inside the bounds of the handle... if you feel like tweaking other parts of the guard object some more, then use EDGE for selection from the modifier stack and tweak away, just stay symmetrical. Here's what my gun looks like...

20. Ok now we are ready to UNION the 2 shapes. By the way, now is a good time to save...

From the creation tab (1st icon with the mouse pointer)... select the "geometry" button which is the sphere icon, then from the drop down list below that find and click "compound objects". Now you have some new options for object creation, click the "BOOLEAN" button. Now make sure that UNION is selected and then press "Pick operand B" now click the main base of the gun. At this stage, your weapon will be triangulated where there is a union. This is fine. And your weapon should look like this...

21. Ok, so now we work on the breach! Hiiii hoooo silver! For this part we are going to be making the ejection hole where the bullet casing flies out, since I don't have a reference for where this is on the gun I'm going to be guessing *cough*... We'll start out from where the extruded part we made earlier ends... this is where the casings will be ejected...

Right so in the creation tab select BOX, now drag out a cube to like in my screener, I didn't have any measurements in mind... you may need to tweak it up with the SCALE and MOVE tools... but as long as you have it looking like in my picture, it'll look fine... Don't forget the Glock18c (like most guns) is a right handed gun and the bullets will be ejected from the RIGHT HAND SIDE! So naturally because of this, your hole will need to be carved out from the underside...

22. Now, the next step will be to Boolean SUBTRACT the two objects, so that the box you just made gets removed from the breach object. But before this can happen the breach needs to be collapsed and made into an Editable mesh. D'you remember how this is done??? ... I just knew you'd say no... uhm, that's fine... so... from the top; go to the modifiers panel, select the top item in your list (will have a light bulb next to it)... and it should be called "extrude" then RIGHT CLICK it and select "collapse to"

Cool if you had no problems there, then... go back to the "creation panel", select "compound object", then "Boolean". Ensure the breach is selected first, then make sure that you have "subtract" selection (A-B) selected!!!, now press "pick operand B" then click the new box object, this is what you get...

23. Ok uh... Gosh... from here, I will just go onto making the trigger and then well, I'll leave it to you model in the small details. I'm gonna also finish up my model with some minor detail tweaks and then move straight onto texturing...

Ok that trigger... this is simple as can be... basically you should outline the trigger in TOP view using the same method when you modelled the other gun parts.

Well, now you are asking why didn't I get you to do it back then? Well, I want you to do it now for practice! So you can see how much you remember! But, when you come to extrude it, make the extrude value 20.0 (same as the trigger guard)... then click the MOVE button and enter into the Z box, 10.0. Come on dude! You know what I'm asking next... Boolean the trigger and the base unit objects together using UNION... when you final pull all your hair out and manage to do it on your own... you get this... (again I say... I HOPE).

24. Ok I know I've been sketchy on the detail modelling, but most of you really should have some basic 3DsMax Knowledge before attempting this tutorial. I mean, I know some of you reading this are novices and that's cool but, the thought of dragging on for another freakin' 3 pages telling you in step by step detail how to produce a simple box or cube is ... err... yeah... well, you get the point. Anyhow, by this time you have hopefully tweaked your weapon and added detail to it... and you'll be ready for texturing.

All I did for detail was carve out the barrel hole from the breach and body, tweaked the trigger, and the trigger guard, then extruded the back end of the handle and added vertices right down the center to make it smoother. I also added a smooth shader, which does nothing to the geometry, just the way a texture appears. And of course the Castle (Aiming sights).

My weapon now comes to a nasty 470 polys. Well, it ain't all that bad... but could probably be reduced further to about 420 polys. Right-o, so let's get texturing, firstly you will need to HAVE a texture to apply to the gun, for now you should just use the background image you outlined earlier. Select both the main part of the gun and the breach, this can by done by using the select tool, from duh, the toolbar (it looks like a mouse pointer)... CLICK the base object, then hold CTRL and CLICK the breach object. Now press "M" on your keyboard and a material window opens up... this is a handy window that allows you to texture shit and make it look schvveeet The window will look like this...

25. The first thing you need to do is click the "Material Type Selector", yeah I know this isn't the proper name for it, but for the life of me, I can't remember what it's really called... err.... so anyway just click it and a second window pops up... I'm HOPING you have the Max Payne KF plug-ins setup properly because this is where it will count... the new window is called "Material Browser"... from this window find "KF Material". This is a material inserted by the plug-in that will allow your model to have the texture you specify and also probably does other stuff like convert mapping co-ordinates to Max Payne 2 standards... DON'T USE ANYTHING ELSE other than "KF MATERIAL"!

26. Double click this material or CLICK OK, now everything seems the same... hmm what has changed? I know! maybe one of the spheres has changed color? No big deal... this is normal. Have a look under the "basic parameters" rollout... and change the diffuse color to white, you can do this by clicking the diffuse color box and using the color map to point at WHITE. When you are done with that, scroll down the material window to the "TEXTURES" rollout, and TICK the diffuse checkbox and then click the little box next to it that says "NONE". Again, the material browser will open... now find and select "KF Texture".

Once you've done that you'll be back in the material window again, have a look at the "basic parameters", AND... the first item there is BITMAP. Click that box and then select your texture... now, the top right hand sphere has the image of the Glock wrapped around it... right? YUP. Also at this point click on the "render texture to viewport button" - if you don't know which one this is, it's labelled in the one of the screeners above...

Cool... now one more step to go before you can apply this texture, look on the material window under the 6 spheres, there should be a drop down box with an eyedropper/pipette next to it... click the drop down box and select "1 - default". Now click on the "Paint material on object" button.

27. Ok, so I mentioned about setting up a map for your gun, we'll do this now. Again with both parts of your gun selected, go to the "modifiers" panel and from the"modifiers list" find, and select "UVW Map"... now as soon as it's selected your gun should show the Glock texture on it properlly... At this stage, it should look something like what I intended... but the texture ISN'T quite right...

The scaling is slightly squashed from handle to breach... so from the modifiers panel have a look for the "UVW Map" item you just added, and under that should be a new item called "Gizmo"... if you can't see it, click on the little PLUS box. Make sure you have BOTH parts of your gun selected, now click the Gizmo item... and a yellow square appears around your model... what you now can do is use the normal SCALE and MOVE tools from the main toolbar to fit the texture as you like it. I suggest maximizing the TOP viewport and turning the view mode to "smooth + highlights" so you can work more accurately... (these options can be accessed in a handy menu by RIGHT CLICKING the TOP LEFT of the active viewport)...

28. Right, so my gun has been textured properly (well to the best I can explain in this tutorial), and if you go back to all 4 viewport windows, select the 3D view; and then press F10, then press the RENDER button... you can see what your model looks like...

29. Ok, so finally, you will notice that the weapon isn't textured as good as it could be (check out the video I put below)... you can improve the quality of the texturing by creating a fitted weapon texture map, and using UVW Unwrap. I'm not gonna explain this since the idea was to just show you how to get started with weapon modelling...

One final thing to note is that most of the detail on the weapon we made is minor, we have modelled the main geometry of the gun and left the detail to the textures.

Well, that's the end of this freakin' long ass tutorial... I really really freakin' hope you have learned a little something about weapon modelling, as I said before it's down to your own imagination and creative genius to produce feasible low poly weapons that look good. There are no map/model downloads for this tutorial, since I know a lot of people will just rip the weapon off for their own mod... there is however a video rendering of the finished weapon (with some added gloss to make it spangly and sexy...)...
